---
title: "On-device transcription"
date: 2026-07-10
category: research
excerpt: "How RECN makes recordings searchable without uploading them for transcription."
---

Audio is difficult to search. The phrase you remember may be hidden forty minutes into a rehearsal.

Transcription solves that. Sending the recording away creates another problem.

So RECN does the work on the device. A small live pass gives words back while you record. A more careful pass follows when the take is safe. The sound does not need to visit a server first.

This took more than adding a model. The engine has to stay warm, decode the real file the recorder creates, work without interrupting capture, and fail honestly when it cannot continue.

The result is straightforward: speak, record, and search the words later.
